# Genre-Style Breakdown (How Survivebest Plays by Genre)

Survivebest is a hybrid. This breakdown shows the major play styles already represented by current systems.

---

## 1) Survival Sim

**Core feel:** Keep your character and household stable day-to-day under pressure.

**Implemented pillars**
- Needs management (hunger/hydration/hygiene/etc. via needs systems).
- Health/medical/status consequences over time.
- Weather and day-slice pressure influencing routine choices.
- Home maintenance/chore pressure affecting household quality.

**Typical survival decisions**
- Eat now vs save resources.
- Rest/treat condition vs keep working.
- Spend money on prevention vs absorb risk.

---

## 2) Social Drama Life Sim

**Core feel:** Relationships, memory, and social choices create emergent drama.

**Implemented pillars**
- Social/dialogue systems.
- Relationship memory and compatibility/tension layers.
- Family/household dynamics and emotional consequences.
- Journal/event feedback loops for social outcomes.

**Typical social decisions**
- De-escalate conflict vs escalate for short-term payoff.
- Invest in family support vs personal ambition.
- Repair trust now vs defer and risk compounding fallout.

---

## 3) Career / Economic Life Sim

**Core feel:** Build stability through jobs, routines, and financial management.

**Implemented pillars**
- Career roster with shifts/pay and workplace metadata.
- Economy + inventory authorities.
- Grocery, ordering, recipes, and household budgeting loops.
- Town services/workforce availability signals.

**Typical economy decisions**
- Buy convenience vs save money.
- Work overtime vs recover needs.
- Skill up now vs prioritize immediate cash.

---

## 4) Crime / Law / Consequence Sim

**Core feel:** Risky actions can trigger legal, social, and financial consequences.

**Implemented pillars**
- Crime/law categories and offense handling hooks.
- Justice outcomes (fines, jail, release lifecycle).
- Substance effects/dependency/withdrawal risk layers.
- Reputation and social memory interactions with misconduct.

**Typical consequence decisions**
- Take illegal shortcut vs legal slower path.
- Pay fine now vs debt pressure later.
- Seek treatment/recovery early vs escalate dependency risk.

---

## 5) Emergent Narrative Sandbox

**Core feel:** Unscripted runs produce unique story arcs from system interactions.

**Implemented pillars**
- Event hub + autonomous story generation.
- AI director pacing over incidents and calm/disruption beats.
- NPC schedule/town simulation + off-screen progression.
- Save/load continuity to continue long-run narratives.

**Typical narrative decisions**
- Lean into chaos for high drama.
- Stabilize systems for long-form legacy play.
- Prioritize one domain (career, family, crime, health) and accept tradeoffs.

---

## 6) Hybrid identity summary

If you want a one-line genre tag:

**“System-heavy life-sim survival RPG with social drama, career/economy pressure, and law/consequence simulation.”**

It is less a linear story game and more a **daily-life consequence engine** where repeated choices shape emergent outcomes.
