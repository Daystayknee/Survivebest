# Pressure-Forward Character Setup Notes

This guide captures a **tense-but-playable life-sim vibe** for creator flows without copying any specific game's language.

## Character Setup Mood Goals

- Build characters who feel **capable but vulnerable**.
- Start with a little friction so early choices matter.
- Keep outcomes understandable: pressure should feel authored, not random punishment.

## Creator Baseline (Vibe-First)

Use these as starting sliders/presets when crafting a new household member:

- **Stress imprint:** medium-high, so routines matter from day one.
- **Cognition/foresight:** medium, to reward planning but still allow mistakes.
- **Appearance confidence:** stable baseline, so setbacks come from life conditions, not immediate identity penalties.
- **Neighborhood context:** practical, lived-in backdrop over sterile studio feel.

## Personality Prompt Starters

When making traits/backstory, prefer contradictions over extremes:

- "Reliable under pressure, avoidant in intimacy."
- "Community-minded, but always one paycheck from panic."
- "Ambitious at work, exhausted at home."
- "Kind in public, brittle in private."

## First-Week Story Hooks

Seed one hook from each column during setup:

1. **Resource Tension**: fridge nearly empty, overdue utility bill, thin transit budget.
2. **Social Tension**: unresolved family expectation, old rivalry, new romantic ambiguity.
3. **Routine Tension**: commute fragility, schedule collisions, low sleep margin.

## Design Guardrails

- Avoid permanent early lockouts.
- Give every pressure source at least one visible counterplay action.
- Prefer chain reactions across 2-3 systems over giant one-shot penalties.
- Ensure at least one "small win" loop is available daily.

## UI/Presentation Direction

- Use concise event language: **what happened -> why -> next best action**.
- Keep visual framing grounded (home/neighborhood/work), not heroic spectacle.
- Emphasize ordinary stakes that escalate into memorable stories.

## Short Checklist

- [ ] Character starts with at least one practical constraint.
- [ ] Character starts with at least one meaningful recovery path.
- [ ] Trait combo implies both strength and vulnerability.
- [ ] First day can produce tension and relief in the same loop.
